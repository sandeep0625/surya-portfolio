Summary

About Me

I am an Electrical Engineering Master’s student at the University of Pennsylvania focused on hardware design, firmware design, embedded systems, and complete system-level debug. I like working close to the hardware — from power rails, sensors, interfaces, and board bring-up to firmware, Linux validation, and lab automation.

My work spans custom embedded boards, FreeRTOS firmware, I2C/SPI/UART/I2S interfaces, ADC signal chains, power telemetry, PCIe/RDMA/CXL/NVMe platform validation, and hands-on debugging using oscilloscopes, logic analyzers, and multimeters.

Outside engineering, I’m deeply interested in Formula 1, automotive systems, robotics, and consumer hardware — products where electronics, software, and mechanical systems come together.
